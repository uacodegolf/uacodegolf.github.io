Challenge 1: "Hello, World?"

Submissions open on 01/16/2020

Submissions close on 01/23/2020 

"Hello, world!" 

It's a greeting to a bright, new day. It's the first program you learn how to write.

A few days back, you were learning a new language. Compilation always takes ages to finish, and you found yourself dozing off in front of your computer screen.

Upon awaking, you noticed a distinct imprint of where your face laid flat onto your monitor. Through your damaged LCD, you saw the output of your program from the night before, streaking down across the screen as your monitor struggles to light up:
  
    Hello, world!
    Hello  world
    Hello  world
    Hello  world
    Hello  worl
    H llo  worl
    H llo  worl
    H llo  worl
      llo  worl
      llo  worl
      llo  worl
      llo  worl
        o  wor
        o  wor
        o  wor
           w r
           w r
           w r
           w
           w
           w
           w
           w

~~~

Write a program which can replicate this output. Your progam must take in a singular line of input, and print it out as if it was smudged downward by a broken monitor.

In the example given, you'll notice that only alphanumeric characters were affected. As it turns out, the amount of times that a character is smudged downwards depends on how far into the alphabet it is. "A" or "a" will not be smudged at all, whereas "Z" or "z" will be smudged 25 additional lines downward. Even though the smudging is case sensitive, it doesn't seem to affect how far certain letters get smudged.

Numbers get smudged as well. "1" escapes unaffected, whereas "2" will be smudged one line downward, all the way up to "9" being smudged 8 lines downward.
